### Highlights

- **Qwen3.6-27B** is a flagship-level dense model that unifies vision–language thinking and non-thinking modes, supports a 1M-token context window, and outperforms its much larger predecessor Qwen3.5-397B-A17B on SWE-bench Verified (77.2 vs 76.2) and Terminal-Bench 2.0 (59.3 vs 52.5). It introduces a "preserved thinking" flag to maintain reasoning context across multi-turn agent tasks.
- **Qwen3.6-35B-A3B** is the open MoE counterpart (35B total, 3B active) optimized for agentic coding while retaining native multimodal perception and reasoning. Performance is competitive with Claude Sonnet 4.5 on coding benchmarks, with the smallest active footprint in its tier.
- **Claude Mythos** is Anthropic's most capable model to date but is **withheld from public release**. Access is restricted to ~50 partner organizations via *Project Glasswing* for defensive cybersecurity and infrastructure vulnerability scanning. This marks the first time a frontier lab has explicitly gated a flagship model on capability/safety grounds.
- **GLM-5V-Turbo** is purpose-built for *vision-to-code*: converting UI mockups, design files, or screenshots into runnable frontend code. It uses a CogViT vision encoder and Multi-Token Prediction to deliver high throughput, with a 200K-token context, designed for integration with OpenClaw and Claude Code agent stacks.

#### Key Insights
- **FreshPER** addresses a previously unrecognized problem in agent RL: trajectory-level prioritized replay accumulates "stale" priorities as the policy drifts, causing the buffer to over-sample no-longer-informative rollouts. A multiplicative exponential age decay restores sample efficiency on agentic tasks (e.g., VLM-FrozenLake), with measurable gains over standard PER and uniform replay.
- **GMPO** stabilizes GRPO training by replacing the arithmetic group mean with a *geometric* mean over token rewards. This down-weights outlier tokens that previously caused gradient explosions, particularly for long-context multimodal generation.
- **ARPO** explicitly attributes credit to individual tool-call sub-trajectories rather than treating the full episode as one unit, matching the structure of multi-turn agent tasks. ICLR 2026.

## Key Trends Observed

1. **Native multimodality crystallizes as the default architecture — but bifurcates on output.** Qwen3.6 (both 27B dense and 35B-A3B MoE), Gemma 4, Gemini 3, and GPT-5.4 are natively multimodal *on input* (early fusion of image / video / audio tokens into one transformer) but still generate **text only**. Models like BAGEL, Qwen3.5-Omni, InternVL-U, Emu3.5, and Erin 5.0 keep that fused trunk and add image / audio **decoder heads** to also generate images and speech. The two-stage *ViT → projector → frozen LLM* design that defined 2023–2024 is now the legacy template; new releases are essentially never built that way. (See the four-panel architecture-evolution diagram at the top of the README — Era 3a vs Era 3b.)

2. **Frontier capability gating arrives.** Anthropic's *Claude Mythos* is the first publicly acknowledged flagship model that is **deliberately not released to the public** — access is restricted to ~50 partner orgs via *Project Glasswing* for defensive cybersecurity. Whether other labs follow sets the tone for the next year of open vs. closed-weight frontier deployment.

3. **Open-weight Chinese labs continue to dominate the agentic-coding tier.** Qwen3.6-27B beats its own much larger Qwen3.5-397B-A17B predecessor on SWE-bench Verified and Terminal-Bench 2.0; GLM-5V-Turbo leads Facts-VLM. Open-weight models now routinely beat proprietary frontier models on agentic coding benchmarks, even if they trail on closed-domain reasoning.

4. **VLA models adopt the "world model + reasoning" recipe.** VLA-World fuses generative world-model rollouts with reflective reasoning trained via GRPO; SpanVLA introduces flow-matching action experts and learns explicitly from failure trajectories; VLA-Forget brings *unlearning* to embodied agents. The driving stack is converging on: predictive imagination + symbolic-style reflection + explicit failure feedback.

5. **Scientific reasoning becomes a first-class VLM benchmark.** S1-VL's *Thinking-with-Images* (executing Python on the image during chain-of-thought) is a qualitative new mode of VLM reasoning. Combined with the new ICLR/Nature position papers on MLLMs for science, scientific image analysis is now a recognized post-training target separate from general visual QA.

6. **The modality gap is now formally measurable — and not closing with scale.** CrossMath isolates the contribution of vision vs. text on identical math problems and shows top frontier VLMs still lean on the textual prior. Combined with BabyVision (where SoTA VLMs fail at toddler-level perception), April's evaluation literature consistently argues that *scaling alone* is not eliminating the gap — a mid-term challenge for the natively-multimodal generation.

7. **RL post-training for VLMs is consolidating around GRPO-family variants with tighter reward design.** GMPO (geometric mean), ARPO (per-tool-call attribution), GRPO-VPS (verifier-proxy signals), TimeSearch-R (chain-search-verify), and GUI-Shift (rule-based + filtered) all build on GRPO with tighter advantage estimation or step-level credit assignment. Combined with FreshPER (priority decay for replay buffers), April marks a clear convergence on "GRPO + careful credit assignment + replay hygiene" as the workhorse recipe.

8. **Efficiency moves from single-axis to joint optimization.** QUOTA shows naive token-pruning + W4A4-quantization stacks are brittle and proposes joint calibration; Fast-dVLM converts AR VLMs into block-diffusion VLMs for >6× decode speedups. Both echo the prior progressive report's QAPruner/Firebolt-VL theme: *vision-token efficiency is now a multi-axis problem* (precision × pruning × decoding paradigm).

9. **Web/GUI agents move past visit-count benchmarks.** WebVoyager is now considered "saturated" at 97–98% for top commercial agents. New benchmarks (WebForge, GameWorld, Mobile-Bench-v2) emphasize *automatically constructed environments* and *adversarial / noisy contexts* (pop-ups, ambiguous instructions). Evaluation is moving from "can the agent do the task?" to "can the agent stay robust?".

10. **Adversarial threats specialize.** SlowBA introduces a new attack surface for VLM-based GUI agents — denial-of-service-style backdoors that degrade *latency* rather than correctness. As agents are deployed in production, attacks are diversifying beyond classical content/jailbreak threats.
