### Highlights

- **The July frontier wave.** Gemini 3.6 Flash, Kimi K3, GPT-5.6 Sol, and Grok 4.5 all shipped between July 8 and July 21 — every one multimodal, every one positioned around agentic/coding workloads with ≥500K context. The 1M-token context window is now table stakes at the frontier (Gemini 3.6 Flash, Kimi K3, GPT-5.6 Sol all at ~1M).
- **Kimi K3** is the boldest open bet: a ~2.8T MoE that runs text, image, and video through one trunk with *no separate vision module*, promised as open weights on July 27. If delivered, it would be the largest open multimodal model by roughly an order of magnitude. On the horizon: Alibaba previewed **Qwen3.8-Max** (2.4T, multimodal) on July 19.
- The **Gemma 4 technical report** documents the family in full — and the notable architectural news is the **12B unified, encoder-free variant**: raw audio and image patches go straight into the trunk with no ViT or audio encoder stage. After Era-3a models dropped the *bridge*, encoder-free designs drop the *encoder* — the strongest signal yet that the tokenizer/encoder stack is dissolving into the backbone itself.
- **Naming caveat for surveyors**: Qwen3.7-**Max** is *text-only* — the multimodal member of that series is Qwen3.7-**Plus**. Model-family names no longer imply uniform modality support; check each tier individually.

## 3. Post-Training / Alignment

> README latest entry: **ZPPO, discrete-diffusion GRPO — 06/2026** (previous progressive report).

| Title | Date | Paper | RL Method | Key Result |
|-------|------|-------|-----------|------------|
| Perceive-to-Reason: Decoupling Perception and Reasoning for Fine-Grained Visual Reasoning | 07/01/2026 | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01191) | **PRA-GRPO** — role-aware RL alternating perception-focused and reasoning-focused updates, final-answer supervision only | P2R-4B (Qwen3-VL base): **93.2% V-Star, 81.9% HR-Bench-4K, 80.5% HR-Bench-8K** |
| Visually Grounded Self-Reflection for VLMs via RL (VRRL) | 07/02/2026 | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02490) | Random trajectory-prefix masking (error recovery) + buffered roll-ins from experience replay (diverse failure states) | Substantially improves **OOD accuracy** on visual grounding and spatial navigation under distribution shift |

#### Key Insights
- **PRA-GRPO** operationalizes at training time what HPP (last cycle) did at inference time: perception and reasoning are different skills that improve under different update signals. Decoupling them inside GRPO — while still using only final-answer rewards — yields a 4B model at 93% on V-Star.
- **VRRL** attacks the *self-reflection* gap: instead of prompting models to "double-check," it trains recovery behavior directly by masking trajectory prefixes and replaying failure states — self-correction as a trained skill grounded in visual feedback, not a prompt pattern.

## Key Trends Observed

1. **A 13-day frontier flood, all multimodal, all agentic.** Gemini 3.6 Flash, Kimi K3, GPT-5.6 Sol, and Grok 4.5 shipped between July 8–21 — every one takes image input, every one is positioned around agentic/coding work, and ~1M-token context is now the frontier norm. Meanwhile the *naming* signal has broken: Qwen3.7-Max is text-only while Qwen3.7-Plus is multimodal, so modality must be verified per tier, not per family.

2. **World models graduate from generator to judge.** GigaWorld-1/WMBench and RoboWorld both use world models as *surrogate evaluators* for robot policies — with RoboWorld hitting r = 0.989 correlation with real-world results. After June's "world model as environment/policy" moment (OmniDreams, Cosmos 3), July adds the third role: the world model as the *benchmark itself*, potentially breaking robotics' real-world-rollout evaluation bottleneck.

3. **Rollout faithfulness beats visual realism.** GigaWorld-1's central finding — evaluation quality depends on long-horizon action-faithful consistency, not short-term visual quality — empirically validates the persistent-state critique from last cycle ("world models lack a persistent state core") and redirects the field's optimization target away from prettier frames.

4. **The encoder is dissolving into the trunk.** Gemma 4's 12B variant is fully encoder-free: raw image patches and audio in, no ViT, no audio encoder. Era 3 dropped the bridge; the frontier is now dropping the encoder — one step closer to a single undifferentiated multimodal transformer.

5. **Perception/reasoning decoupling moves from inference to training.** PRA-GRPO alternates perception- and reasoning-focused RL updates (93.2% V-Star at 4B), following HPP's inference-time decoupling and the TOC-Bench/Video-Oasis diagnosis that monolithic models conflate the two failure modes. The perception/reasoning split is becoming a design principle across the stack.

6. **Self-correction becomes a trained, visually-grounded skill.** VRRL trains error recovery directly — masking trajectory prefixes and replaying failure states — rather than prompting for reflection, and the gains concentrate exactly where deployment needs them: out-of-distribution shift.

7. **VLA data scaling continues its 6× -per-month climb.** Hy-Embodied reported 10k hours (June 12), Qwen-RobotManip 38k (June 16), LingBot-VLA 2.0 now ~60k hours across 20 embodiments (July 7). Meanwhile ROSA brings *serving infrastructure* for robot factories — the industrial MLOps layer that signals VLA deployment is leaving the lab.

8. **Video-LLM hallucination taxonomy sharpens.** MoHallBench isolates *motion* hallucination (40k QA pairs) and finds sequential-inference errors dominate; LongVQUBench shows quality understanding collapses with length. Combined with prior cycles' TOC-Bench and VideoZeroBench, the video evaluation stack now probes object identity, motion, spatial evidence, and duration independently.

9. **Efficiency work converges on "absorb, don't cache."** ISPA bakes video history into model weights (50% KV-cache cut); OmniFocus compresses tokens per-modality by query relevance. Both extend the June theme (FastOCR, WindowQuant) that multimodal inference cost is a *representation* problem, not just a kernel problem.

10. **Frontier–open gap reappears on expert visual literacy.** The scientific-visualization study puts Gemini above the human mean while all open models sit below it — a reminder that aggregate benchmark parity hasn't closed the gap on specialized visual-expertise tasks, and that texture/quantitative-estimation skills remain uniformly weak.

11. **The Era-4 framing gets its textbook.** The "From World Models to World Action Models" tutorial codifies the world-model → world-action-model transition into four paradigms — the same taxonomy this repo adopted in its architecture figure — signaling the framing has moved from observation to consensus.
