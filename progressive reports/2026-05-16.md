### Highlights

- **LensVLM** introduces a novel paradigm: instead of OCR or text extraction, it *renders text as images* and uses selective context expansion to process documents. At 4.3× effective compression, it matches full-text accuracy on document QA; at 10.1× compression it still outperforms baselines. This re-frames text-heavy understanding as a vision problem.
- **Nemotron 3 Nano Omni** is NVIDIA's first omni-modal model: vision, audio, and text in a single 30B-A3B MoE. It leads open-model leaderboards on document intelligence (MMlongbench-Doc, OCRBenchV2), video understanding (WorldSense), and audio (VoiceBench, DailyOmni). Delivers **9× higher throughput** than comparable open omni models with a 256K-token context and Conv3D-based temporal video compression.
- **LLaDA2.0-Uni** is the first unified **discrete diffusion** LLM for both multimodal understanding and generation. Instead of autoregressive decoding, it uses masked diffusion with 8-step inference via distillation. Matches specialized VLMs on understanding benchmarks while also supporting image generation, editing, and interleaved reasoning — a fundamentally different generation paradigm from the AR-dominant field.
- **PLaMo 2.1-VL** targets Japanese-language edge deployment (2B/8B), filling a niche for non-English compact VLMs with VQA and visual grounding capabilities.

## 3. Post-Training / Alignment

### 3.1 RL Alignment for VLM / VLA

> README latest entry: **Vero — 04/2026**; previous report added **FreshPER, GMPO, ARPO, GRPO-VPS, TimeSearch-R, GUI-Shift**.

| Title | Date | Paper | RL Method | Code |
|-------|------|-------|-----------|------|
| S-GRPO: Unified Post-Training for Large VLMs | 04/2026 | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16557) | Supervised GRPO — injects ground-truth trajectories into candidate group to solve cold-start/optimization collapse | — |
| Faithful GRPO (FGRPO): Constrained Policy Optimization for Visual Spatial Reasoning | 04/2026 | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08476) | Lagrangian-constrained GRPO; reduces inconsistency rate from 24.5% → 1.7% | — |
| GRPO-TTA: Test-Time Visual Tuning via GRPO-Driven RL | 05/05/2026 | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03403) | Adapts GRPO for test-time visual encoder tuning; no ground-truth labels needed | — |
| OpenSearch-VL: Multi-Turn Fatal-Aware GRPO for Multimodal Search Agents | 05/07/2026 | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05185) | Fatal-aware GRPO that handles tool-call failures in agentic multi-turn RL | — |

#### Key Insights
- **S-GRPO** solves a critical practical problem: unaligned VLMs fail to generate valid outputs for GRPO to optimize over (the "cold-start" problem). By conditionally including ground-truth trajectories in the candidate group, it guarantees positive advantage signals and prevents optimization collapse — a simple but essential fix for applying GRPO to foundation models out-of-the-box.
- **Faithful GRPO** enforces logical consistency and visual grounding as hard constraints via Lagrangian dual ascent, rather than folding them into the reward. The dramatic drop in inconsistency (24.5% → 1.7%) while maintaining accuracy gains demonstrates that *constrained* RL is more reliable than reward-shaping alone for spatial reasoning.
- **GRPO-TTA** extends GRPO beyond training to *test-time adaptation* — dynamically tuning the visual encoder at inference without any labeled data, using alignment and dispersion rewards. This is conceptually novel: RL as a deployment-time technique, not just a training-time one.
- **OpenSearch-VL** tackles a practical failure mode in agentic RL: tool calls that fail (broken links, timeouts, API errors). Its "fatal-aware" GRPO variant avoids penalizing the policy for environmental failures, which is essential for real-world search agents.

## Key Trends Observed

1. **Non-autoregressive generation enters the VLM mainstream.** LLaDA2.0-Uni demonstrates that discrete diffusion LLMs can match specialized autoregressive VLMs on understanding while also generating images — a fundamentally different paradigm from the AR-dominant field. Combined with Nemotron 3 Nano Omni's MoE omni-modal approach, May 2026 marks the first time multiple non-traditional generation paradigms compete directly with AR at scale.

2. **GRPO proliferates from training to test-time to failure handling.** S-GRPO solves the cold-start problem, Faithful GRPO adds hard constraints, GRPO-TTA adapts at inference time, and OpenSearch-VL handles tool-call failures. The GRPO family has expanded from a single RL recipe into a **full spectrum of optimization tools** covering every stage of the VLM lifecycle — pretraining warm-up, alignment, deployment adaptation, and agentic robustness.

3. **Data curation proves more cost-effective than architecture or scale.** DatologyAI's "20/20 Vision" study demonstrates +11.7pp improvement from curation alone at 17–87× less compute, while the Synergy Dilemma paper shows that stacking CoT SFT and RL doesn't reliably combine. The field is shifting from "train more" to "curate better" — an inflection point for compute-efficient VLM development.

4. **Video understanding benchmarks face an existential reckoning.** Video-Oasis reveals that 54% of existing benchmark samples are solvable without visual input, while VideoZeroBench shows even the best models achieve <1% accuracy when spatial and temporal evidence is required simultaneously. The gap between "video QA scores" and "genuine video understanding" is now empirically quantified and alarming.

5. **Autonomous driving VLAs surpass human performance.** MindVLA-U1's unified streaming architecture achieves 8.20 RFS vs 8.13 for experienced human drivers on WOD-E2E — the first VLA to demonstrably exceed human-level driving performance on a standard benchmark. Combined with RAG-augmented long-tail handling (VLADriver-RAG) and unified heterogeneous decoding (OneDrive), the driving VLA stack is maturing from research demos to deployment-ready systems.

6. **SFT understanding deepens — mid-layer localization and the RL gap.** The layer-wise analysis shows SFT changes concentrate in middle transformer layers (20%–80%), enabling targeted mid-block tuning that beats LoRA by 10.2%. Meanwhile, DC-SFT demonstrates that RL's generalization advantage comes from implicit difficulty-aware sample weighting — suggesting that smarter data sampling can close the RL/SFT gap without the complexity of RL training.

7. **VLA frameworks consolidate for real-world deployment.** VLA Foundry provides a unified open-source pipeline, Green-VLA introduces a five-stage curriculum with zero-shot cross-embodiment transfer, and Anticipation-VLA tackles long-horizon planning via recursive subgoal generation. The VLA ecosystem is transitioning from one-off model papers to reusable, modular frameworks suitable for production robotics.

8. **Medical VLMs hit a multi-image reasoning wall.** MedThinkVQA reveals that even frontier models (Claude-4.6-Opus, Gemini-3-Pro) achieve only ~55% accuracy on multi-image clinical cases, with 70%+ of errors traced to image reading and cross-view integration rather than reasoning. This quantifies a deployment-critical bottleneck: clinical medicine requires synthesizing information across multiple views/scans, and current VLMs cannot do this reliably.

9. **Text-as-vision challenges the OCR paradigm.** LensVLM's approach of rendering text as images and processing it visually — matching full-text accuracy at 4.3× compression — suggests that the boundary between "text understanding" and "vision understanding" is more permeable than assumed. If text can be efficiently processed as visual input, the traditional text-encoding pipeline may become optional for document-heavy workflows.

10. **GUI agents scale down to 3B while gaining exploration autonomy.** LAMO enables 3B models to perform GUI automation via role-based orchestration, while ScreenExplorer introduces curiosity-driven autonomous GUI exploration without predefined tasks. Combined with InfiGUIAgent's hierarchical reasoning, GUI agents are simultaneously getting smaller, more autonomous, and more capable — a rare triple win.
