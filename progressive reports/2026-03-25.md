### Highlights

- **GPT-5.4** introduces a 1M-token context window, native computer-use abilities, and configurable reasoning effort (none → xhigh). 33% fewer per-claim errors vs GPT-5.2; 83% on GDPval.
- **Phi-4-Reasoning-Vision-15B** pushes the Pareto frontier of accuracy vs. compute for compact models. Excels at math/science reasoning and GUI understanding via hybrid reasoning/non-reasoning data with explicit mode tokens.
- **Gemini 3.0** features a 10M-token context window and native cross-modal reasoning across text, images, audio, and video in a unified latent space.
- **Qwen3.5** is a unified VL foundation model trained with early fusion on trillions of multimodal tokens. Outperforms Qwen3-VL across reasoning, coding, agents, and visual understanding.
- **Claude Opus 4.6** supports 1M-token context with up to 600 images/PDF pages per request. Emphasis on structured visual content, document analysis, and safety.
- **Molmo2** achieves SoTA among open-weight models for video understanding and grounding. Its 8B model outperforms Gemini 3 Pro on video pointing (38.4 vs 20.0 F1) and tracking (56.2 vs 41.1 J&F).

| SaFeR-VLM: Safety-Aware RL for Multimodal Reasoning | 2026 (ICLR) | [Paper](https://openreview.net/pdf/4f379d45027946b58a820908fd3a1711d66daa85.pdf) | GRPO + safety reward | - |

#### Key Insights
- **f-GRPO** extends the divergence-based alignment perspective to verifiable-reward RL (RLVR), providing a unified framework for on-policy (f-GRPO) and hybrid on/off-policy (f-HAL) alignment.
- **wDPO** uses hierarchical winsorization to distinguish hard noise from ambiguous comparisons, replacing DPO's uniform regularization.
- **GIFT** unifies GRPO, DPO, and UNA by minimizing implicit-explicit reward model discrepancy, achieving faster convergence with fewer hyperparameters.
- **SaFeR-VLM** embeds safety directly into RL training; SaFeR-VLM-3B outperforms Qwen2.5VL-72B on safety metrics.

## Key Trends Observed

1. **Unified multimodal architectures dominate**: Qwen3.5, GPT-5.4, and Gemini 3.0 all trend toward native multimodal fusion rather than bolted-on vision encoders. Early fusion training on interleaved modalities is becoming standard.

2. **Context windows are exploding**: GPT-5.4 at 1M tokens, Gemini 3.0 at 10M tokens, Claude Opus 4.6 at 1M tokens. Long-context video and document understanding are direct beneficiaries.

3. **RL alignment is diversifying beyond GRPO**: New methods like f-GRPO (divergence-based), wDPO (winsorized), GIFT (GRPO+DPO+UNA hybrid), and dual-reward systems (SAIL-RL) address specific failure modes of vanilla GRPO/DPO.

4. **SFT is not dead**: New evidence (arXiv:2512.12690) shows SFT achieves comparable reasoning performance to RL with 10× less data, especially for smaller models. Methods like MFT and MERGETUNE address catastrophic forgetting.

5. **Safety-aware training is maturing**: SaFeR-VLM and HoliSafe represent a shift from post-hoc safety evaluation to safety-integrated training and holistic benchmarking.

6. **Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models proliferate**: DAM-VLA, NS-VLA, ST4VLA, HiST-VLA, and AutoMoT show rapid progress in bridging VLM reasoning with physical robot control and autonomous driving.

7. **Compact models compete with giants**: Phi-4-Reasoning-Vision-15B and H2OVL-Mississippi demonstrate that carefully curated data and architecture choices allow sub-20B models to rival much larger systems on specific tasks.

8. **Hallucination detection goes proactive**: HALP detects hallucinations from internal representations before any text is generated, enabling early abstention and adaptive decoding.

9. **Open-weight video VLMs are catching up**: Molmo2 (8B) outperforms proprietary models like Gemini 3 Pro on video grounding tasks while being fully open-weight with open training data.

10. **Quantization enables massive efficiency gains**: GRACE achieves INT4 models that outperform FP16 baselines with 3× throughput; VLMQ shows 16.45% improvement even at 2-bit quantization.
