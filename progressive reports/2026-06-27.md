### 1.1 World-Model Foundation Releases

| Model | Date | Type | Scale / License | Key Result |
|-------|------|------|-----------------|------------|
| [Cosmos 3 (NVIDIA)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02800) | 06/01/2026 | **Omnimodal** world-model family — language, image, video, audio, and **action** in one mixture-of-transformers | Family of models; code, checkpoints, data, and evals under **OpenMDW-1.1** | SoTA across vision-language, video generation, and robotic policy: rated best open-source **T2I and I2V** models and **best policy model on RoboArena** |
| [Kairos](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16533) | 06/15/2026 | "Native world model stack" for Physical AI — unified understanding + generation + prediction | **4B** unified architecture; Hybrid Linear Temporal Attention (sliding + dilated windows + gated linear attention) | Native pre-training via a **cross-embodiment data curriculum** (open-world video → human behavior → robot interaction); real-time rollout on consumer/edge hardware; **beats 14B models** on embodied benchmarks |
| [DreamX-World 1.0 (Alibaba AMAP)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16993) | 06/15/2026 | General-purpose **interactive** text/image-to-video world model | **5B** (Wan2.2-T2V-5B base); **MIT-licensed** open weights ([HF](https://huggingface.co/GD-ML/DreamX-World-5B), [GitHub](https://github.com/AMAP-ML/DreamX-World)) | Controllable long-horizon generation with camera navigation, **revisits to previously seen regions**, and promptable events; E-PRoPE camera-aware positional encoding; causal forcing + DMD distillation turn a bidirectional generator into a **few-step autoregressive** world model, with long-rollout training suppressing drift |

#### Highlights

- **Cosmos 3** is the biggest release the earlier cuts missed: NVIDIA folds understanding, generation, *and action* into one omnimodal family, and it wins on both content-generation leaderboards **and** RoboArena policy evaluation — the clearest instance yet of a single world model serving as generator, perceiver, and policy. Notably it lands just two weeks before its own sibling OmniDreams (06/02 report), showing NVIDIA running the world-model stack at two altitudes: foundation family (Cosmos 3) and domain simulator (OmniDreams).
- **Kairos** pushes the opposite direction — *compactness*: a 4B unified world model with linear-attention hybrids that runs real-time on edge hardware and still beats 14B baselines. Its framing ("world models as operational infrastructure, not passive visual generators") is becoming the field's consensus position.
- **DreamX-World 1.0** is the strongest *open* interactive world model to date (MIT license, 5B): it directly targets the revisit-consistency and long-rollout-drift problems that WorldOlympiad and the persistent-state critique (§5) identify as core weaknesses.

## 2. Post-Training / Alignment

> README latest entry: **discrete-diffusion GRPO (2606.14792) — 06/2026** (previous progressive report).

| Title | Date | Paper | Method | Key Result |
|-------|------|-------|--------|------------|
| ZPPO: Zone of Proximal Policy Optimization — Teacher in Prompts, Not Gradients | 06/16/2026 | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18216) | Teacher guidance injected via **reformulated prompts** (correct teacher + incorrect student responses on hard questions), not gradient-level distillation | On **Qwen3.5 at 4 student scales (0.8B–9B)** with a 27B teacher, post-trained as VLMs: **outperforms off/on-policy distillation and GRPO** across a 31-benchmark suite (16 VLM, 10 LLM, 5 video) |

#### Key Insight
- **ZPPO** reframes teacher-student distillation for VLM post-training: instead of matching teacher logits or trajectories, it builds Vygotsky-style "zone of proximal development" prompts that contrast teacher successes with the student's own failures — and beats both distillation and GRPO across scales. Prompt-space supervision is emerging as a cheaper, more robust alternative to gradient-space transfer.

## Key Trends Observed

1. **World-model foundation releases arrive in force.** In one month: Cosmos 3 (omnimodal family, action included), Kairos (4B edge-real-time stack), DreamX-World 1.0 (5B MIT-licensed interactive model) — plus OmniDreams from the prior cut. World models have moved from research demos to *release-grade artifacts with licenses, checkpoints, and leaderboard wins*, mirroring where VLMs were in 2023.

2. **One model, three roles: generator, perceiver, policy.** Cosmos 3 simultaneously tops open T2I/I2V generation and RoboArena *policy* evaluation, and Kairos unifies understanding/generation/prediction in one 4B trunk. The boundary between "world model" and "VLA policy" is dissolving — consistent with OmniDreams' world-action model beating a VLA at 1/5 the parameters last cut.

3. **Persistent state is named as the core missing piece.** The "tracking shot" critique shows world models freeze off-screen entities rather than evolving them, across all architectures and scales; Echo-Memory finds state-space recurrence (not bigger context) best preserves revisited scenes; DreamX-World engineers explicitly for revisit consistency. The field has converged on the diagnosis: world models need a *decoupled internal state*, not better renderers.

4. **Compact + edge is a deliberate world-model design point.** Kairos targets real-time rollout on consumer hardware with hybrid linear attention at 4B (beating 14B) — extending the previous cut's efficiency theme (VLA layer pruning, RT-VLA) from policies to the world models themselves.

5. **Robot pretraining corpora jump an order of magnitude.** Qwen-RobotManip's ~38,100-hour aligned corpus (vs Hy-Embodied's 10,000 hours two weeks earlier) shows manipulation data scaling accelerating, with alignment across representation/motion/behavior as the unlock — echoing the "alignment, not just scale" thesis from VLM training.

6. **Synthetic robot video finds its lane: geometry, not actions.** The Asymmetric Preservation Principle (geometry survives video generation; control signals don't) gives a principled recipe for what synthetic data can and cannot supervise — resolving a debate that pseudo-action approaches had been losing empirically.

7. **Prompt-space beats gradient-space for VLM distillation.** ZPPO's teacher-in-prompts outperforms both distillation and GRPO across Qwen3.5 scales, suggesting the cheapest channel for capability transfer in post-training may be constructed context, not gradients.

8. **Process rewards go visual.** VisCritic scores GUI agent steps by comparing screenshots rather than text rationales — process supervision grounded in observable state change, aligning with the prior cut's "reasoning consistency as safety signal" (Lost in Fog) in using model-external evidence over self-report.

9. **Perception/reasoning decoupling wins again in long video.** HPP's code-LLM-probes-VLM architecture beats end-to-end models, joining last month's benchmark findings (TOC-Bench, Video-Oasis) that monolithic video LLMs conflate perception failure with reasoning failure.

10. **Medical safety findings escalate from reading to dialogue.** IMCBench shows frontier models' safety erodes exactly on malignant/rare conditions in multi-turn patient conversations (best score 3.61/5) — after HalluCXR showed 62–82% hallucination in radiograph reading. The clinical-deployment gap is now documented at both the perception and interaction layers.
