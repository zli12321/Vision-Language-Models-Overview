### 1.1 Generative World Models & Simulators

| Model | Date | Type | Backbone / Data | Key Result |
|-------|------|------|-----------------|------------|
| [NVIDIA OmniDreams](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03159) | 06/02/2026 | Real-time generative world model for closed-loop AV simulation | Mid/post-trained from **Cosmos** diffusion on **21k hours** of driving | Reactively generates action-conditioned sensor video; a world-action model derived from it **beats the VLA-based Alpamayo 1.5 policy on NuRec using 1/5 the parameters** |
| [Mirage — Latent Spatial Memory for Video World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09828) | 06/08/2026 | Memory-augmented video world model | Diffusion latent space (no explicit RGB memory) | Stores 3D scene memory by lifting latent tokens into 3D via **depth-guided back-projection** and querying through latent-space warping — faster generation with far lower memory than explicit-3D baselines |
| [Reward as An Agent for Embodied World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19990) | 06/18/2026 | RL post-training for world models | DynDiff-GRPO + agentic reward | Replaces conservative rollouts with **explicit action-space exploration**; agentic reward verification curbs reward hacking, improving accuracy across multiple open-source embodied world models |

#### Highlights

- **OmniDreams** is the headline result this cycle: a foundation generative world model that runs **closed-loop, action-conditioned, in real time** for autonomous-vehicle simulation. By mid/post-training NVIDIA's Cosmos on 21k hours of driving, it synthesizes long-tail phenomena (extreme weather, unusual agent behavior) that reconstruction-based neural simulators cannot — and a policy distilled from it outperforms a VLA baseline at 1/5 the parameters. This is a concrete sign of world models displacing both classical simulators *and* direct VLA policies for driving.
- **Mirage** attacks the central cost of long-horizon video world models — memory — by keeping 3D scene state **in diffusion latent space** rather than re-rendering pixels, trading explicit 3D reconstruction for depth-guided latent warping. It is a representative of the broader move toward *latent* spatial memory (see also LongSpace in §1.2).
- **Reward as An Agent** brings the GRPO lineage that dominated VLM alignment into **world-model training**, where physical plausibility and task completion provide a hard, verifiable reward — and explicitly diversifies exploration instead of staying near the training distribution.

|-------|------|-------|-----------|------------|
| Efficient Reinforcement for Visual-Textual Thinking with Discrete Diffusion | 06/11/2026 | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14792) | GRPO over a **multimodal discrete diffusion** model; visual rollouts via **localized visual editing** instead of full image-token regeneration | Cuts GRPO rollout computation by **26.9%** vs autoregressive baselines with minimal performance drop for interleaved visual-textual reasoning |

#### Key Insight
- Following last cycle's LLaDA2.0-Uni and AlphaGRPO, **discrete diffusion** keeps gaining ground as an RL substrate: because diffusion can edit a region locally rather than re-emit the whole image-token sequence, rollouts (the dominant GRPO cost) get cheaper — a structural efficiency advantage AR generation cannot easily match.

## Key Trends Observed

1. **World models go real-time and closed-loop.** NVIDIA OmniDreams turns Cosmos into a real-time, action-conditioned driving simulator whose distilled policy beats a VLA at 1/5 the parameters. The world model is no longer just a video generator — it is becoming the *environment* that policies are trained and evaluated in, and sometimes a better policy source than direct VLA imitation.

2. **Spatial memory moves into the latent space.** Mirage stores 3D scene memory as warped diffusion latents (depth-guided back-projection) rather than explicit reconstruction or RGB caches, while LongSpace shows video MLLMs still fail at long-horizon recall without dedicated spatial memory. Memory — not raw generation quality — is emerging as the binding constraint on long-horizon world models.

3. **World-model evaluation gets a reckoning of its own.** WorldOlympiad's physics/geometry/interaction "triathlon" finds SoTA world models break down on physical plausibility, 3D consistency, and sustained controllability — the same "scores ≠ understanding" pattern that hit video QA benchmarks in prior cycles, now arriving for generative world models.

4. **GRPO crosses into world-model training.** "Reward as An Agent" applies an agentic-reward GRPO variant (DynDiff-GRPO) to embodied world models, using physical plausibility as a verifiable signal and explicitly diversifying exploration. The GRPO family continues its march — from VLM reasoning to test-time adaptation (earlier cycles) to, now, dynamics learning.

5. **Discrete diffusion is structurally cheaper for multimodal RL.** Localized visual editing lets diffusion-based reasoners cut GRPO rollout cost ~27% versus autoregressive regeneration — a recurring advantage (LLaDA2.0-Uni → AlphaGRPO → this) suggesting non-AR generation may win on the *training-efficiency* axis even where AR matches quality.

6. **Specialist VLMs keep beating giants on narrow tasks.** A 34.5M-parameter PP-OCRv6 surpasses 235B-class VLMs (Qwen3-VL-235B, GPT-5.5, Gemini-3.1-Pro) on OCR, reinforcing the FastOCR lesson: for dense-document and other bounded tasks, task-specific design dominates brute-force multimodal scale.

7. **VLA efficiency comes from depth, not just decoding.** "Fewer Layers Than You Think" shows VLAs carry ~50% redundant depth that CKA-based pruning removes for 40–50% faster training and 30% faster inference, while RT-VLA's distillation hits 44.8× speedups for driving. After last cycle's throughput-focused driving VLAs, efficiency work is now targeting the *backbone* itself.

8. **Language becomes a control surface for robots — safely.** "Learning What to Say to Your VLA" searches for and distills language that steers closed-loop behavior (+65% on hardware) with explicit safeguards against harmful OOD steering, framing natural-language instruction as a tunable, auditable control channel rather than a fixed prompt.

9. **3D grounding spreads across VLMs.** Occ-VLM grounds language tokens in a reconstructed occupancy field for indoor scenes, extending the VLM3 "native 3D" thread from previous cycle — VLMs are increasingly expected to reason in reconstructed 3D space, not just on flat pixels.

10. **Embodied AI consolidates into full stacks.** Hy-Embodied-0.5-VLA packages backbone, flow-matching action expert, a 10,000-hour egocentric corpus, and RL post-training into one data-to-deployment pipeline — continuing the prior-cycle shift (VLA Foundry, Green-VLA) from single-model papers to reusable, production-oriented robot-learning systems.
